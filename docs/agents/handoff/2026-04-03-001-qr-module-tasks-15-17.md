# Key decisions

- Kept signed-only QR tokens instead of encrypted tokens.
  - Reason: explicit user choice earlier in the branch.
  - Trade-off: token payload remains decodable, but implementation is simpler and aligned with earlier work.
- Kept the original custom scan architecture instead of integrating with ERPNext native barcode scan.
  - Reason: native barcode scanning is a poor fit for workflow-token dispatch; user explicitly chose to drop the exploration and keep the original plan.
  - Trade-off: less framework-native UI reuse, but cleaner fit for QR workflow actions.
- Task 15 final lifecycle decision:
  - Generate QR on `Quality Inspection.submit()` exactly as the plan says.
  - Do not gate QR generation on `Purchase Receipt.docstatus` in the hook.
  - Enforce lifecycle safety in downstream handlers instead.
  - Trade-off: users may scan a QR before the PR is submitted and get a controlled validation error; this preserves ERPNext flow and spec compliance.
- Task 16 testing approach:
  - Covered `after_install()` wiring in a focused hook-level test rather than deep integration around `create_custom_fields()`.
  - Trade-off: narrower test, but stable and sufficient for the wiring change.
- Task 17 Purchase Invoice quantity logic:
  - Initial custom quantity math was rejected in review because it diverged from ERPNext behavior for partial billing, returns, and rejection settings.
  - Final handler defers pending-quantity and return semantics to ERPNext’s native `make_purchase_invoice` mapper.
  - Trade-off: tighter coupling to ERPNext internal behavior, but correct lifecycle behavior and much lower maintenance risk.
- Branch hygiene decision during this session:
  - `.agents/skills/handoff/` was intentionally left untracked and excluded from commits/pushes.
  - Reason: unrelated to the ASN implementation work.
